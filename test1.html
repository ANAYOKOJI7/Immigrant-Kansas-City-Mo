Assistance and Support Offices at Kansas City

Helping Residents Access Essential Services

In Kansas City, various offices provide assistance and support to help residents meet their needs. From food and healthcare programs to housing and employment services, these resources ensure that everyone can access the help they deserve.

. Department of Social Services (DSS) – Family Support Division (FSD) These offices provide access to food assistance (SNAP), temporary financial aid (TANF), subsidized child care, Medicaid (MO HealthNet), energy assistance (LIHEAP), and employment/training programs. They also serve as the main entry point for other state benefit programs. Social Security Administration (SSA) The SSA manages Social Security Numbers (SSN), retirement benefits, disability benefits, and other federal payments. Local offices assist with new applications, updates, and replacement cards. 📞 National Line: 1-800-772-1213 (TTY: 1-800-325-0778) 🔗 ssa.gov 3. MO HealthNet (Missouri Medicaid) MO HealthNet is Missouri’s public health insurance program. It covers doctor visits, dental care, mental health, prescriptions, non-emergency medical transportation (NEMT), and medical equipment. Eligible groups include low-income individuals, children, pregnant women, people with disabilities, and seniors. 📞 DSS Line: 1-855-373-4636 📞 Health Coverage: 1-800-392-2161 📞 Managed Care Plans: 1-800-348-6627 📞 Medical Transportation: 1-866-269-5927 🔗 mydss.mo.gov 4. Community Action Agency of Greater Kansas City (CAAGKC) CAAGKC fights poverty by providing food assistance, utility bill support (LIHEAP), weatherization programs, housing help, and job/life skills training. It serves Jackson, Clay, and Platte counties.
